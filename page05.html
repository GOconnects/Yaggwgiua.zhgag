Introduction to the Keto Diet

The ketogenic (keto) diet is a high-fat, low-carb eating plan designed to put your body into a state of ketosis, where it burns fat for fuel instead of carbohydrates. Itâ€™s popular for weight loss, improved energy, and managing conditions like type 2 diabetes.

How the Keto Diet Works

  • Macronutrient Breakdown: 70-75% fat, 20-25% protein, 5-10% carbs.
  • Ketosis: When carb intake is low, the body produces ketones from fat, which become the primary energy source.

Types of Keto Diet Plans

  • Standard Keto Diet (SKD): Classic high-fat, low-carb plan.
  • Cyclical Keto Diet (CKD): Alternates between keto and higher-carb days.
  • Targeted Keto Diet (TKD): Allows small amounts of carbs around workouts.
  • High-Protein Keto Diet: Similar to SKD but with more protein.

Benefits of the Keto Diet

  • Weight Loss: Burns fat efficiently.
  • Improved Energy: Stable energy levels without sugar crashes.
  • Better Mental Clarity: Ketones are a preferred fuel for the brain.
  • Blood Sugar Control: Helps manage type 2 diabetes.

Potential Challenges

  • Keto Flu: Temporary side effects like headaches and fatigue.
  • Nutrient Deficiencies: Risk of missing key nutrients.
  • Sustainability: Can be difficult to maintain long-term.

Foods to Eat and Avoid

  • Eat: Avocados, olive oil, eggs, chicken, spinach, almonds.
  • Avoid: Bread, pasta, rice, sugary snacks, starchy vegetables.
